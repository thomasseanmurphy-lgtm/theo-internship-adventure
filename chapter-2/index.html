📖 The story

Read me first!

Good news! A recruiter has just phoned Théo: the company wants to meet him for an interview on Friday. Théo is excited but nervous. He has never had a real job interview before.

First, he researches the company online. The firm has grown a lot since 2010, and last year it opened a new hub near the highway. Its trucks are used all over Europe, and thousands of packages are sent every day. Théo takes notes to remember the key facts.

Then he prepares for the big day. He chooses smart clothes for the interview and prints his CV to show his skills. His teacher says: "You should research the company, and you mustn't be late!"

On Friday, Théo is interviewed by the manager. Here is part of their conversation:

Manager: "Why do you want this internship?"
Théo: "I want it because your warehouse is very modern."
Manager: "What are your strengths?"
Théo: "I am organized and I work hard."
Manager: "When can you start?"
Théo: "I can start in June."
Manager: "Great! We are happy to offer you the job."
Théo: "Thank you very much!"

The manager was happy with his answers — and Théo is hired! 🎉

Interview advice (modals)

8 pts

Conseil, obligation, interdiction ou pas nécessaire ? Choisis le modal qui a le bon sens.

  1. It's a good idea: you research the company before the interview.
  2. It is strictly forbidden: you be late — arrive early!
  3. It's a rule: you bring your ID card to enter the building.
  4. Never do this in an interview: you lie about your skills.
  5. The dress code is relaxed, so you wear a tie.
  6. It's obligatory: you turn off your phone during the interview.
  7. It's only advice: you speak too fast.
  8. It's optional: at the end, you ask a few questions if you want.
